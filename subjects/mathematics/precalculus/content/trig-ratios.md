## Introduction

Trigonometry, derived from the Greek words *trigonon* (triangle) and *metron* (measure), deals with the relationships between the sides and angles of triangles. It was initially developed in ancient Greece and India to study astronomy and geometry. Today, trigonometric ratios are fundamental in various fields such as engineering, physics, and computer science. The three primary trigonometric functions — sine (sin), cosine (cos), and tangent (tan) — help us understand the relationship between the angles and sides of right triangles. These ratios, along with their reciprocals — cosecant (csc), secant (sec), and cotangent (cot) — form the basis of much of trigonometry.

---

## The Content

In a right-angled triangle, trigonometric ratios are defined as follows:

1. **Sine (sin θ)** – the ratio of the opposite side to the hypotenuse:  
   $\sin \theta = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{hypotenuse}}$

2. **Cosine (cos θ)** – the ratio of the adjacent side to the hypotenuse:  
   $\cos \theta = \frac{\text{adjacent}}{\text{hypotenuse}}$

3. **Tangent (tan θ)** – the ratio of the opposite side to the adjacent side:  
   $\tan \theta = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{adjacent}}$

### Reciprocal Functions

1. **Cosecant (csc θ)** – the reciprocal of sine:  
   $\csc \theta = \frac{1}{\sin \theta} = \frac{\text{hypotenuse}}{\text{opposite}}$

2. **Secant (sec θ)** – the reciprocal of cosine:  
   $\sec \theta = \frac{1}{\cos \theta} = \frac{\text{hypotenuse}}{\text{adjacent}}$

3. **Cotangent (cot θ)** – the reciprocal of tangent:  
   $\cot \theta = \frac{1}{\tan \theta} = \frac{\text{adjacent}}{\text{opposite}}$

## Worked Examples

### Example 1

Given a right-angled triangle where the opposite side to the angle θ is 3 and the hypotenuse is 5, find sin θ, cos θ, and tan θ.

**Solution:**  
Using Pythagoras’ theorem, the adjacent side is calculated as:  
$
\text{adjacent} = \sqrt{5^2 - 3^2} = \sqrt{25 - 9} = \sqrt{16} = 4
$  
Now we can find the trigonometric ratios:  
$
\sin \theta = \frac{3}{5}, \quad \cos \theta = \frac{4}{5}, \quad \tan \theta = \frac{3}{4}
$

### Example 2

Find the reciprocal ratios for θ in the previous example.

**Solution:**  
$
\csc \theta = \frac{5}{3}, \quad \sec \theta = \frac{5}{4}, \quad \cot \theta = \frac{4}{3}
$

### Example 3

In a right-angled triangle, if $\tan \theta = 2$, find $\sin \theta$ and $\cos \theta$.

**Solution:**  
We know:  
$
\tan \theta = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{adjacent}} = 2
$  
Let the adjacent side be $k$, so the opposite side is $2k$. The hypotenuse is:  
$
\text{hypotenuse} = \sqrt{(2k)^2 + k^2} = \sqrt{4k^2 + k^2} = \sqrt{5k^2} = k\sqrt{5}
$  
Therefore:  
$
\sin \theta = \frac{2k}{k\sqrt{5}} = \frac{2}{\sqrt{5}}, \quad \cos \theta = \frac{k}{k\sqrt{5}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{5}}
$  
Rationalizing the denominators:  
$
\sin \theta = \frac{2\sqrt{5}}{5}, \quad \cos \theta = \frac{\sqrt{5}}{5}
$

## Solutions

1. $$\cos \theta = \sqrt{1 - \sin^2 \theta} = \sqrt{1 - 0.36} = 0.8$$  
   $$\tan \theta = \frac{\sin \theta}{\cos \theta} = \frac{0.6}{0.8} = 0.75$$

2. $$\cos \theta = \frac{\text{adjacent}}{\text{hypotenuse}} = \frac{7}{25} = 0.28$$  
   $$\sin \theta = \sqrt{1 - \cos^2 \theta} = \sqrt{1 - 0.0784} = 0.96$$  
   $$\tan \theta = \frac{\sin \theta}{\cos \theta} = \frac{0.96}{0.28} \approx 3.4286$$

3. Using opposite = 3k, adjacent = 4k, hypotenuse = 5k:  
   $$\sin \theta = \frac{3}{5}, \quad \cos \theta = \frac{4}{5}, \quad \tan \theta = \frac{3}{4}$$  
   $$\csc \theta = \frac{5}{3}, \quad \sec \theta = \frac{5}{4}, \quad \cot \theta = \frac{4}{3}$$

4. $$\csc \theta = \frac{1}{\sin \theta} = \frac{5}{3}$$

5. $$\sec \theta = \frac{1}{\cos \theta} = \frac{13}{5} = 2.6$$  
   $$\sin \theta = \sqrt{1 - \cos^2 \theta} = \frac{12}{13}$$  
   $$\cot \theta = \frac{\cos \theta}{\sin \theta} = \frac{5/13}{12/13} = \frac{5}{12}$$

6. Adjacent side: $$\sqrt{10^2 - 6^2} = 8$$  
   $$\sin \theta = \frac{6}{10} = 0.6$$, $$\cos \theta = \frac{8}{10} = 0.8$$, $$\tan \theta = \frac{6}{8} = 0.75$$  
   $$\csc \theta = \frac{10}{6}, \quad \sec \theta = \frac{10}{8}, \quad \cot \theta = \frac{8}{6}$$

7. Hypotenuse: $$\sqrt{8^2 + 15^2} = 17$$  
   $$\sin \theta = \frac{8}{17} \approx 0.4706$$  
   $$\cos \theta = \frac{15}{17} \approx 0.8824$$  
   $$\tan \theta = \frac{8}{15} \approx 0.5333$$

8. $$\csc \theta = \frac{1}{\sin \theta} = \frac{4}{5}$$  
   $$\sec \theta = \frac{1}{\cos \theta} = \frac{5}{3}$$  
   $$\cot \theta = \frac{3}{4}$$

9. Opposite side: $$\sqrt{15^2 - 9^2} = 12$$  
   $$\sin \theta = \frac{12}{15} = 0.8$$  
   $$\cos \theta = \frac{9}{15} = 0.6$$  
   $$\tan \theta = \frac{12}{9} = 1.3333$$

10. $$\sin \theta = \frac{1}{\csc \theta} = \frac{3}{5}$$

11. Opposite side: $$\sqrt{1^2 + 7^2} = \sqrt{50}$$  
    $$\sin \theta = \frac{7}{\sqrt{50}} = \frac{7\sqrt{2}}{10} \approx 0.9899$$  
    $$\cos \theta = \frac{1}{\sqrt{50}} = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{10} \approx 0.1414$$

12. Opposite side: $$\sqrt{13^2 - 5^2} = 12$$  
    $$\sin \theta = \frac{12}{13}, \quad \cos \theta = \frac{5}{13}, \quad \tan \theta = \frac{12}{5}$$  
    $$\csc \theta = \frac{13}{12}, \quad \sec \theta = \frac{13}{5}, \quad \cot \theta = \frac{5}{12}$$

13. Using $$\sin \theta = \frac{3}{5}$$ and $$\cos \theta = \frac{4}{5}$$:  
    $$\sin^2 \theta + \cos^2 \theta = \left(\frac{3}{5}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{4}{5}\right)^2 = \frac{9}{25} + \frac{16}{25} = 1$$

14. $$\cos \theta = \frac{1}{\sec \theta} = \frac{5}{13}$$  
    $$\sin \theta = \sqrt{1 - \cos^2 \theta} = \frac{12}{13}$$  
    $$\tan \theta = \frac{\sin \theta}{\cos \theta} = \frac{12}{5} = 2.4$$

15. $$\cos \theta = \sqrt{1 - \sin^2 \theta} = \sqrt{1 - 0.64} = 0.6$$

16. Sides are 3, 4, 5:  
    $$\sin \theta = \frac{3}{5}, \quad \cos \theta = \frac{4}{5}, \quad \tan \theta = \frac{3}{4}$$

17. $$\csc \theta = \frac{1}{0.75} = \frac{4}{3}$$  
    $$\cos \theta = \sqrt{1 - \sin^2 \theta} = 0.66, \quad \sec \theta = \frac{1}{\cos \theta} \approx 1.5$$  
    $$\cot \theta = \frac{\cos \theta}{\sin \theta} \approx 0.88$$

18. $$\cot \theta = \frac{\cos \theta}{\sin \theta} = \frac{\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}}{\frac{1}{2}} = \sqrt{3}$$

19. Adjacent side: $$\sqrt{10^2 - 6^2} = 8$$  
    $$\sin \theta = \frac{6}{10} = 0.6$$  
    $$\cos \theta = \frac{8}{10} = 0.8$$  
    $$\tan \theta = \frac{6}{8} = 0.75$$

20. Using right triangle ratios:  
    $$\tan \theta = \frac{\sin \theta}{\cos \theta}$$  
    (Proven)
